Goa VAT refunds remain a critical process for businesses with excess Input Tax Credit (ITC), TDS credits, or appeal pre-deposit balances, governed by the Goa Value Added Tax Act, 2005, and Rules, 2005 (updated through 2026 amendments). The procedure, verified as accurate from official rules like Rule 30 and Forms VAT-XII/XVI, ensures thorough checks while mandating timelines to prevent undue delays. As of March 2026, no substantive changes alter this post-assessment/appeal flow despite minor composition scheme tweaks.

When Refunds Arise
Refunds trigger from finalized assessment orders (e.g., excess ITC over output liability) or appeal outcomes (e.g., balance pre-deposit after liability revision). Common scenarios include overpaid tax, unutilized ITC from zero-rated exports/inter-state sales, or TDS mismatches. File transfer to verification authority occurs automatically based on quantified refund amount.

Detailed Step-by-Step Procedure

1. Initial File Transfer and Authority Allocation
Post-order, the jurisdictional Commercial Tax Officer (CTO) quantifies the refund and routes the file:
•	≤ ₹50,000: Managed directly by CTO via scrutiny.
•	₹50,001 to ₹2 lakhs: Requires sanction from Assistant/Deputy Commissioner of State Tax.
•	> ₹2 lakhs: Escalates to Additional Commissioner of State Tax (currently centralized in Panjim).
Verification involves cross-checking returns, audit trails, invoices, TDS certificates, and appeal adjustments for genuineness.

2. Refund Order Issuance
Upon confirmation, the officer issues a formal refund order specifying amount, basis, and date. The department must credit funds within 3 months from order date or pay 8% p.a. simple interest under Section 33(2) pro-rata for delays—calculated daily from day 91. Assessee can claim interest via application if unpaid.

3. Jurisdictional Review for Adjustments
File returns to the original CTO, who scans for outstanding demands (e.g., prior years' arrears, penalties).
•	If dues exist: Issues Form VAT-XVI (Refund Adjustment Order), sets off refund against liabilities (oldest first), and vouches balance.
•	No dues: Proceeds directly to voucher.
Submit bank details promptly cancelled cheque preferred for IFSC/MICR validation to avoid rejection.

4. Refund Voucher Preparation
CTO generates multiple copies of Form VAT-XII (Refund Voucher):
•	Details: Assessee name, TIN, amount, bank info, order reference.
•	Copies: One to SBI Treasury Branch (Panjim), one (or more) to assessee, duplicates for records.

5. Treasury Submission Requirements
Contact SBI Treasury Branch (Panjim) to confirm receipt of departmental copy. Then submit assessee’ s copy in person with:
•	Revenue stamp.
•	Company seal (if applicable).
•	Authorized signatory's full signature.
•	Bank letter on letterhead certifying account ownership, activity, and matching details.

6. Final Crediting
Bank verifies documents (1-2 days), processes payment, and credits within 7 working days. Receive SMS/confirmation; reconcile in next return.

For direct ITC refunds (pre-assessment, Rule 9A), apply via Form VAT-IX with shorter 30-day scrutiny—but post-order process applies identically. Always retain copies; consult your CA for case-specifics. Latest at statetax.goa.gov.in.
